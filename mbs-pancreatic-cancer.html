Pancreatic Cancer: AJCC 8th Edition Staging
Staging drives the MBS item pathway. Clinical resectability classification is often more operationally relevant than pathological stage.
Stage I

Resectable

  • T1–T2, N0, M0
  • Tumour ≤4 cm, confined to pancreas
  • No major vessel involvement
  • No regional node or distant mets
Stage II–IIB

Borderline Resectable

  • T3 or N1 (1–3 nodes), M0
  • Tumour >4 cm or minor vessel abutment
  • Limited regional nodal involvement
  • Neoadjuvant chemo often given
Stage III

Locally Advanced

  • T4 or N2 (≥4 nodes), M0
  • Major vessel encasement (SMA, coeliac)
  • Unresectable without neoadjuvant Rx
  • Systemic chemo ± SBRT first line
Stage IV

Metastatic

  • Any T, Any N, M1
  • Liver, peritoneum most common mets
  • Palliative intent chemotherapy
  • Biliary palliation (stenting) key

Resectable Disease — Stages I & II
Goal: curative resection. Pathway includes EUS staging/FNA, surgical resection (Whipple's or distal pancreatectomy), adjuvant chemotherapy, surveillance imaging.
Endoscopy — Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS) (Category 3 – T8)
EUS is the preferred modality for tissue diagnosis (FNA/biopsy) and locoregional staging. It is claimable when there is strong clinical suspicion of pancreatic neoplasia, including when CT is negative but clinical suspicion is high. NOT claimable for routine surveillance of chronic pancreatitis without clinical suspicion of malignancy.

STAGE III
Locally Advanced Disease — Stage III
Goal: systemic disease control, potential downstaging for resection. Key MBS categories: chemotherapy, biliary palliation, radiation planning, restaging.
Locally advanced pancreatic cancer (LAPC) is typically unresectable due to encasement of the superior mesenteric artery, coeliac axis, or portal vein. First-line treatment is systemic chemotherapy (FOLFIRINOX preferred where performance status allows), with selective use of SBRT or chemoradiation. After 4–6 months, restaging determines conversion to resectability in ~10–15% of cases.

Molecular & Genetic Testing — Stage IV
Molecular profiling is increasingly important in metastatic pancreatic cancer. BRCA1/2 somatic or germline mutations (~5–7%) predict response to olaparib (POLO trial). NTRK fusions, RET alterations, and TMB-high are rare but actionable. KRAS G12C inhibitors are in clinical trials.

Understanding Repeat Median

The repeat median in this document refers to the estimated clinical frequency with which each MBS item is likely to be claimed for a pancreatic cancer patient within a treatment episode. This is not the same as the Medicare Item Report "repeat" figure, which describes the median number of times an item is billed per patient per year across all patients claiming that item. The Medicare Item Reports (Services Australia) provide quarter-by-quarter statistics on services rendered, patients, providers, and fee data. These are publicly available at Services Australia Medicare Item Reports.

Key MBS Rules for Pancreatic Cancer Items
Multiple Operation Rule (MOR): When two or more Category 3 T8 surgical items are billed at the same sitting, the second item is paid at 50% and subsequent items at 25% of schedule fee. This applies to co-claimed items such as 30584 (Whipple's) + 30800 (splenectomy).
Hospital vs Non-Hospital Items: Items marked (H) can only be claimed for in-hospital patients (private or public). EUS items 30688–30694 are (H) items. CT and pathology items are available both in and out of hospital. MBS benefit = 75% for in-hospital; 85% for out-of-hospital bulk-billed.
EUS Co-claiming: EUS items (30688–30694) can be co-claimed with interventional ERCP items (30484, 30485, 30491, 30494) on the same day, same patient, same provider since the 2021 MBS reforms. This allows combined diagnostic/therapeutic procedures.
PET Scan (From Jul 2025): Items 61612 and 61614 have been expanded to cover any FDG-avid cancer including pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, removing the need for specific cancer type approvals. Neuroendocrine tumours of the pancreas may alternatively use DOTATATE PET (item 61650) as they are somatostatin receptor-avid.
